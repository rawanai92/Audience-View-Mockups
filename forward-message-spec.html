Forward a message — the full experience

The complete forward experience for Conversations, beat by beat: what the agent sees and does inside the 24-hour window (the everyday flow), and how it adapts the moment that window is closed. Grounded in what the platform can actually do today, with the new build clearly marked.

The one thing to know first: forwarding is a re-send, and the "Forwarded" tag is ours alone

WhatsApp has no native forward. We re-send the original content as a new message. So the customer receives a normal-looking message — Meta does not let businesses show a "Forwarded" label. The label and the "Forwarded from…" provenance live only in our agent inbox. Everything below builds on that one fact.

1The everyday flow — forwarding inside the 24-hour window

This is the experience to live through first: a message is open, the customer is active, and the agent wants it in another conversation. Beat by beat — exactly what they see and do.

1
Find the message → ⋯ → Forward
Hovering any text or media bubble reveals a borderless ⋯ action on the bubble's edge. Opening it shows Reply · Copy · Forward.
You see: the ⋯ appears only on hover, so it never clutters the thread.
You do: click Forward. (Templates, interactive/button messages and reactions don't offer Forward — see §2.)
2
"Forward to…" opens — with a preview of exactly what's being sent
A focused panel shows the message being forwarded at the top (the text, or the media + its caption), then a searchable list of conversations.
You see: a faithful preview so you're sure you grabbed the right message; below it, your conversations.
You do: search or scroll, then pick one destination. The picker shows every conversation in your workspace — open or closed window — nothing is hidden (see §3).
3
(Media only) tweak the caption
For an image / video / document, the caption is editable before sending. The file itself is re-used from our storage — the agent never re-uploads.
You see: the original caption, pre-filled and editable.
4
The truth pill confirms what'll happen → Forward
A status pill in the footer states the outcome before you commit. For an open window it reads Sends instantly.
You see: a single, honest "this is what happens" line — the same truth-pill pattern the composer uses.
You do: click Forward.
5
Delivered — and tagged in our inbox
The view switches to the destination conversation. The forwarded message appears with a grey "↪ Forwarded" label and a "Forwarded from {sender}" line; a toast confirms "Forwarded to {name}".
You see: the message delivered (✓✓), labelled and attributed — for your team only.
The customer sees: a normal message. No forwarded tag, no provenance (see headline).

3The destination picker

One small surface, a few firm rules — it's where "forward to whoever you need" is honoured.

Shows everyoneEvery conversation in the tenant — open and closed windows alike. A closed one isn't hidden; it's labelled "Window closed · 24h elapsed" and the flow adapts (§5).
One destinationv1 forwards to a single conversation. Bulk / multi-select is out.
Same tenant onlySource and destination are always the same workspace. No cross-tenant, no cross-channel (e.g. IG → WA).

4Two views of the same forward — ours vs. the customer's

The single most important thing for agents to internalise. We can be rich internally; the customer just gets the content.

In our agent inbox

Labelled & attributed

  • Grey italic "↪ Forwarded" label on the destination bubble
  • "Forwarded from {Contact / Agent}" provenance line
  • We keep: original sender, source conversation, original time
  • Marked team-only — visible to agents, tenant-scoped
  • Jump-to-original from the line → Phase 2 (static text in v1)
On the customer's phone

A normal message

  • No "Forwarded" badge (Meta doesn't expose it to businesses)
  • No provenance — they never see who it came from
  • Just the text, or the media + caption
  • Agent-facing copy must never promise "they'll see it was forwarded"

5Forwarding after the 24-hour window

When the customer has been quiet >24h, WhatsApp only delivers approved templates. The agent can still pick that conversation — the flow adapts and the truth pill tells the truth. Forwarding reopens the chat by sending the content as a one-off template.

How a one-off template works (it already exists for text)

The content is auto-wrapped into a single-use WhatsApp template, submitted to Meta, and delivered the moment it's approved (usually minutes) — which reopens the conversation. The template is for that send only and is auto-deleted ~24h later. If an already-approved template matches the content, it's reused instantly.

What the agent experiences, by type

A
Text → one-off text template Queued · reopens chat
Picks the closed conversation; the panel explains the reopen and the ~24h auto-delete.
You do: "Send as one-off". You see: a pending bubble — flips to delivered once Meta approves.
B
Image / Document / Video → one-off media template Queued · reopens chat
The file rides in the template header; a short agent-editable line becomes the body (default: "Hi 👋 here's the file you asked about.").
You see: a "Customer receives: [your file] + the line above" preview, and a clear note that the original caption is replaced by that line.
You do: edit the line if you like → "Send as one-off".
C
Audio / voice note → blocked Not available
WhatsApp has no audio template header, so there's no honest way to deliver it post-window.
You see: a clear reason, plus a workaround: send a text to reopen the chat; once the customer replies, forward the voice note. Optional "🔔 notify me when they reply".

Two honesty points to message everywhere

1. Post-24h is not instant — it waits on Meta approval (usually minutes); the bubble shows pending until delivered.   2. For media, what the agent sees ≠ what the customer receives — the editable line replaces the caption. Both are shown in the panel before the agent commits.

8Acceptance criteria

AC1⋯ menu shows Forward on text + media (image/video/document/audio); not on templates/interactive/reactions.
AC2Forward panel shows a preview + a searchable list of the tenant's conversations, open and closed alike; one destination selectable.
AC3For media, caption is editable; the media is re-used (agent never re-uploads).
AC4A footer truth pill states the outcome before send: Sends instantly / Queued · reopens / Not available.
AC5Inside 24h: any supported content forwards instantly; destination bubble shows "Forwarded" + "Forwarded from {sender}".
AC6Closed · text: reuses the one-off text template — queued → delivered on approval → reopens → auto-deleted.
AC7Closed · image/document/video: one-off media-header template (file = header, editable line = body); panel shows "Customer receives [file] + line" and the caption-replacement note.
AC8Closed · audio: blocked with reason + reopen workaround + optional notify; nothing sent.
AC9Provenance is internal-only — never sent to Meta or the customer.
AC10Source and destination are the same tenant; no cross-tenant / cross-channel.

9Decisions & open questions

Decided for v1

Locked

  • Destinations: any conversation, open or closed
  • Closed window: text + image + document + video reopen via one-off template; audio blocked
  • Media body: short, agent-editable line (replaces caption)
  • Who can forward: all agents (no role gate)
  • Provenance: full "Forwarded from {sender}", team-only
Open

Needs a call

  • Build sequencing: text+image first, then document+video — together or staged?
  • Default body line per type; may the agent leave it blank?
  • Rejection UX: how a Meta-rejected one-off is surfaced & retried
  • Audio "notify me when they reply" — real capability or just a message?
  • Privacy: hide the original customer's identity across customer chats?

Judge it — the review rubric

  • Legible — before I send, do I know exactly what the customer will receive (especially post-24h)?
  • Honest — does the truth pill ever over-promise (e.g. imply instant when it's queued)?
  • Unblocked — can I forward to whoever I need, or do I hit a wall I don't understand?
  • Familiar — does it feel like the conversations inbox I already use, or a new thing to learn?
  • Safe — is provenance clearly team-only, with no risk of leaking it to the customer?
